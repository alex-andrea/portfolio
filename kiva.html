Website Redesign / May 2018

I worked in a group of three to evaluate and redesign the Kiva website. Over one and a half weeks we conducted preliminary user research in the form of surveys, identified the major points of friction for Kiva's users, and redesigned the website to improve usability.

kiva.com
← screens from Kiva's current website

An infographic I made to show Kiva's microlending process: You can lend as little as $25 to an entrepreneur in need, who will use the donation to grow their business. Because it's a loan, not a donation, the entrepreneur will then pay back the loan, allowing you to relend the same $25 to another entrepreneur.

We used UsabilityHub to test our wireframe against a wireframe of IKEA's current layout. We gave users instructions to find a specific IKEA product. Our layout resulted in a 1779.43% increase in task completion compared to IKEA's current layout.
